Birthplace:

I was born in Saigon (Ho Chi Minh), Vietnam in Oct 2002.


The above image is a baby picture (clearly), and the one to the right is when I was roughly 3 years old

What was it like?

Well, I lived there until I was roughly 8 before moving to America. During that time I did go to kindergarden and remember very faint experiences of then. I grew up speaking Chinese (Cantonese) along with Vietnamese.


The above image to the left is my kindergarden graduation photo from Vietnam haha!

My brother (in the fish hat) and I (on the outer most left side) were at the airport, ready to board the plane to go to Allentown, Pennsylvania (September 9, 2009).

First School!

I moved to Allentown, Pennsylvania and started the school in the firstgrade roughly around end of Fall. I really did not understand the English language at all during that year but I feel like I was still able to get by communicating with simple phrases/terms.


I remember being in ELL (English Language Learning) classes for the majority of my time there where I was able to meet a few others like me who did not speak any English at all.


I still found it fun being able to spend time with new friends!

My First Winter

I was in Pennsylvania near the beginning of Winter and I've never experienced anything like this sort of weather. I was astonished by snow and especially how much it snowed, I may have been very small at the time but I confidently want to say that it snowed more than a foot.


My brother and I spent so many hours over one weekend playing outside in the snow and we became very sick for a week afterwards. I did not regret that experience, and I still enjoy Winter and snow very much to this day!

High School Experience

With each elevation in school from primary to intermediate, and secondary. There are some significant shifts in the environment for sure. From the more vast variety of sports and clubs to being able to leave campus for lunch, and the harder cirriculums of AP and IB where it can contribuite to your potential future. I was very excited for sure being able to choose more of the classes I like and also in meeting new people.


My name here was not wasted at all, I spent quite the amount of time exploring around extracirrular activities after school that were offered. I found myself joining many sports team as well as recreational clubs.


I expecially enjoyed joining the track team, swim team, as well as ASC (Asian Student Coalition) club at Hazen. I even ran a club with some of my friends, swhich was extremely hard as well as fun!

2021 Graduation

Four years, has never felt so long...........


Our Junoir and Senoir year was basically robbed from us, having to isolate for over a year and moving to online school was something that was never expected. However, complaints about then is for another story. I'm very glad to have made it out now and slowly moving on from that horrendous experience of online schooling.


My friends and I have never been so excited to see each other once again and especially during the time leading up to our graduation. Lots of us have been deciding on our post high school plans and cannot wait to see it. Some of us are sad that this may be the last time that we see each other for a while. Overall, I also had mixed feelings of excitment as well sadness. But this was something that has to happen so it is ok!
